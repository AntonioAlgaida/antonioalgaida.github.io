PhD student in computer science, artificial intelligence, autonomous driving and wireless communication at the Polytechnic University of Cartagena, Murcia, Spain. Currently I am less than 6 months away from finishing my PhD, while I am doing a pre-doctoral stay at the University of California, Davis.During my PhD focused on artificial intelligence, autonomous driving and telecommunication systems engineering, I have developed an intelligent system that controls autonomous vehicles in a decentralized way, using 6G mobile communication technology. Thanks to deep multi-agent reinforcement learning (MADRL), the system is able to control vehicles autonomously, taking into account all vehicles at the intersection. Exploring new frontiers of knowledge within the field of MADRL is a challenge that I would love to tackle in my professional and personal development. Likewise, within the field of artificial intelligence, I find the field of time-series prediction, meta-learning, imitation-learning, zero-shot-learning and genetic algorithms, in addition to signal processing, natural language processing and computer vision, etc., really exciting.
As you can see, both in my researchgate and in my github, I am passionate about the field of artificial intelligence (deep learning, reinforcement learning, multi-agent systems) and research, as well as data science. I have published several papers related to this field, as well as presented results at conferences such as NeurIPS. Throughout my PhD I have developed great programming skills in python, in addition to having knowledge in C++ and java, developed during my telecommunications engineering.
In addition, in my github page you can find several projects focused on artificial intelligence, deep learning and deep learning by reinforcement such as Vocals2Song, a project to, from the singer's audio wave, generate a song (based on pix2pixhd); EdgeSRGAN a super-resolution project to improve object detection algorithms by helping edges and using generative adversarial networks, among others, and several competitions I have participated in such as MineRL 2021 by NeurIPS, and Flatland2021.
Personally, I consider myself a friendly, sociable and passionate about the world of research and group work, with enormous potential. I believe that a lot of progress has been made in the world of science when working in groups and I think there is still a lot to develop to achieve a better, fairer and more equitable world.
